Why I'm Here

I've been managing a local shop for a great company in the dying print industry for the last 8 years.

My career was going nowhere fast. I'd already moved up as much as I was going to for the foreseeable future, and $40K was just not enough to keep me there for 10 more years.

I made the decision last year to move on, strike out and find a better way forward. In a growth industry. It's like going to work for Ford after you quit your job at the Horse & buggy factory.

A friend talked me up on taking up web development. I haven't looked back since that day.

I choose DBC because it's the best. Unlike traditional education where a degree is a degree is a degree, @ DBC you only get a set of dog tags and some kick ass training.

My thinking is if I'm not getting a piece of paper that carries serious weight then you should want the best education you can get. DBC is the best in this field, their students are the best and I will be the best developer I can be as a result of that environment.

I don't love code. I don't live for it. I'm not there yet. But much like a 16th century royal marriage, I will grow to love it in the years to come. Our love will be stronger because I will have to work to make it work, gaining knowledge and insight with each passing day.. We just need to get to know one another a bit more before I go throwing out the "L" word. ( Smiles )
